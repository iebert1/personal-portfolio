lil 'bout me

Ok, how do I do this?
Let's see... I grew up in Soviet Яussia. So, that was pretty awesome! I got to learn some essential survival skills - like how to grow beets and reuse empty pickle jars. (Yes, Russian stereotypes are true, at least some of them! )

Also, when I was little I wanted to be a cashier! I played alone - imaginary customers, an oversized calculator. I felt important smashing plastic buttons. But the best part was adding/subtracting large numbers in my head. I had fun with it! At that age I didn't have to learn "that kinda math" - I just wanted an efficient solution. Later in my math class - I HAD to do it. That's when the fun ended and I didn't want to play with numbers anymore.

Fast-forward 25 years » I'm in my 30s and live in the US. There have been many things I had to do - be serious and organized, get a college degree and work a government job. I think I managed well. Most of all I love all the things I have done so far - write, dance, travel, play piano, adopt dogs, paint, garden, climb trees, ride horses, live in pAris, swim with dolphins, explore art, read French, speak Latin, teach children, teach myself.

I'm always learning something new and, as of recent, I've been diving deeper in Digital Photography. I don't have to - I want to. So, in the future, I want to share my photography here.



Thank you for reading,

Irina
