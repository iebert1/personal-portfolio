Code Book

lil 'bout me

Ok, how do I do this?
Let's see... I grew up in Soviet Russia. So, that was pretty awesome. I got to learn some essential survival skills - like how to grow beets and reuse empty pickle jars! (Yes, Russian stereotypes are true, at least some of them! )

Also, when I was little I wanted to be a cashier! I played alone - imaginary customers, an oversized calculator. I felt important smashing plastic buttons. But the best part was adding/subtracting large numbers in my head. I had fun with it! At that age I didn't have to learn "that kinda math" - I just wanted an efficient solution. Later in my math class - I HAD to do it. That's when the fun ended and I didn't want to play with numbers anymore. Not for awhile anyway.

Fast-forward 25 years » I'm in my early 30s and live in the US. There have been many things I had to do - be serious and organized, get a college degree and work a government job. It was ok. I managed well. But most of all I loved all the things I wanted to do - write, dance, travel, play piano, adopt dogs, paint, garden, climb trees, swim with dolphins, fall in love, read French, speak Latin, teach children, teach myself.

Now, I'm learning to code. I don't have to - I want to. I have unofficially enrolled myself in a bootcamp of CREATIVE DISCPLINE. I practice daily and the practice saved my life from a broken heart. Literally. But most importantly the more I learn to code, the more I want to be quicker at finding simpler solutions, be more spatially aware. Last night I was dreaming of custom checkboxes and submit forms. It was a weird dream but when I got up I went straight to my laptop and fixed a bug!

... Ok, so, I don't know for sure where I'll be or what I'll do a year from now but regardless I'll be grateful if I do what I want and not what I have to, at least half the time.

Thank you for reading,

Irina
